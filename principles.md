# Principles

## Why This System Exists

You're running a company. You're making high-stakes decisions. You're managing people, capital, time, and uncertainty.

And yet, you probably don't have a system for managing yourself.

This system exists to give you that: **a structured way to reflect, decide, and act with clarity.**

## Core Principles

### 1. Clarity Over Complexity

More dashboards don't create more insight. More meetings don't create more alignment. More tools don't create more progress.

Clarity does.

This system prioritizes **thinking time over tracking time**. It asks the right questions, not every question.

### 2. Reflection, Not Performance Theater

This is not a system to show your board, your team, or your investors. This is for you.

No one else will see these files unless you choose to share them.

Write honestly. The goal is self-awareness, not self-promotion.

### 3. Accountability Without Surveillance

You don't need an app to tell you you're behind. You already know.

What you need is **space to notice why** and **permission to adjust**.

This system holds you accountable to your values and priorities, not to arbitrary metrics or someone else's definition of success.

### 4. Action Follows Insight

The point of reflection is not reflection. It's **better action**.

Every review, every interview, every framework should surface at least one decision or adjustment.

If a practice doesn't lead to clarity or change, stop doing it.

### 5. Systems Beat Willpower

You will not remember to reflect unless it's scheduled.
You will not notice patterns unless you write them down.
You will not course-correct unless you review regularly.

This system doesn't rely on motivation. It relies on rhythm:
- Daily check-ins (5 minutes)
- Weekly reviews (20 minutes)
- Quarterly evaluations (2 hours)
- Annual reflection (1 day)

Protect these rhythms. They're the leverage points.

### 6. Trust Yourself

You know what matters. You know what's working and what isn't. You know where you're avoiding hard decisions.

This system doesn't tell you what to do. It asks you what you already know, and creates space for you to act on it.

### 7. Flexibility Over Rigidity

If a prompt doesn't land, skip it. If a framework doesn't fit, adapt it. If a practice feels like bureaucracy, kill it.

This system is a tool, not a religion. Use what works. Ignore the rest.

## What This System Is Not

- **Not a task manager.** Use Asana, Notion, or a notebook for tasks. This is for thinking, not tracking.
- **Not therapy.** This system helps you reflect on your work and life, but it's not a substitute for professional support if you need it.
- **Not a habit tracker.** You won't get points or streaks. You'll get clarity.
- **Not a dashboard.** There are no charts, no analytics, no vanity metrics. Just you and the questions that matter.

## How To Use These Principles

Come back to this file when the system starts to feel like a chore.

If you're doing this out of obligation rather than insight, something's wrong.

Adjust the system. Skip what doesn't serve you. Add what does.

The goal is clarity, not compliance.

---

*Principles are only useful if they change behavior. If these don't, write your own.*
